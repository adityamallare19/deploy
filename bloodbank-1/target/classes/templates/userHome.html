Red Blood Cells

Red blood cells (RBCs) are essential components of blood that carry oxygen from the lungs to the rest of the body. They contain hemoglobin, a protein that binds with oxygen and gives blood its red color. RBCs play a crucial role in maintaining proper oxygen levels throughout the body, ensuring the health and functionality of tissues and organs.

Donation Details:

  • One unit of whole blood donation
  • Donors can typically donate every 8 weeks

Plasma Donation

Plasma is the liquid portion of blood that contains water, electrolytes, and proteins, including antibodies and clotting factors. Plasma donation involves collecting plasma through a process called apheresis. This donation method allows the extraction of specific blood components, with the remaining components returned to the donor. Plasma is crucial for treating various medical conditions, including burn injuries and clotting disorders.

Donation Details:

  • Plasma donation through apheresis
  • Donors can typically donate every 4 weeks

Platelet Donation

Platelets are small cell fragments in the blood that play a vital role in blood clotting and wound healing. Platelet donation is achieved through apheresis, where platelets are separated from the blood, and the remaining components are returned to the donor. Platelet donations are often used to support cancer patients, individuals undergoing surgeries, and those with certain blood disorders. Donors are encouraged to contribute regularly due to the short shelf life of platelets.

Donation Details:

  • Platelet donation through apheresis
  • Donors can typically donate every 7 days, but it is more common to donate every 2 to 4 weeks
